2. Scope of this Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, store, share, and protect personal data of users who visit our website at qureprotein.com ("Website"), purchase our products, subscribe to our newsletter, or otherwise interact with QURE. It is compliant with the GDPR and the Polish Act of 10 May 2018 on the Protection of Personal Data (Journal of Laws of 2019, item 1781, as amended).

3. Personal data we collect

Depending on how you interact with us, we may collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Identification and contact data: first name, last name, email address, phone number (if provided), shipping and billing address.
  • Order data: products ordered, order value, payment method (we do not store full payment card numbers), delivery details.
  • Account data: login, password (hashed), order history, preferences (if you create an account).
  • Communication data: content of messages sent via the contact form, email correspondence, customer service tickets.
  • Marketing data: newsletter subscription, consent records, marketing preferences.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type and version, device type, operating system, language, time-zone, referring URL, pages visited, cookie identifiers — collected via cookies and similar technologies (see our Cookies Policy).

4. Purposes and legal basis of processing

We process your personal data only when we have a valid legal basis under Article 6(1) GDPR:

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR) — to process and deliver your orders, manage subscriptions, handle returns and complaints, and operate your account.
  • Compliance with a legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR) — to issue invoices, fulfil tax and accounting duties, respond to lawful requests from public authorities, and retain documentation required under Polish accounting law for 5 years.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) — to send you marketing communications such as our newsletter, to use non-essential cookies, and for any other processing for which we have specifically asked for your consent. You may withdraw your consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.
  • Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) — to ensure the security of our Website, to defend against potential claims, to conduct anonymised analytics, to handle correspondence sent to us outside of order context, and to pursue our own marketing of similar products to existing customers (so-called "soft opt-in"). You may object to processing based on legitimate interest at any time.

5. Recipients of personal data

We share personal data only with carefully selected entities that need it to perform their services for us, and only on the basis of written data processing agreements pursuant to Art. 28 GDPR. Recipients may include:

  • Courier and logistics companies (to deliver your orders);
  • Payment service providers (to process transactions);
  • IT and hosting providers (to operate the Website and our internal systems);
  • Email and marketing platform providers (to send transactional and marketing messages);
  • Accounting and tax advisors (to fulfil bookkeeping and tax obligations);
  • Legal advisors (where necessary to assert or defend our rights);
  • Public authorities, where required by law.

Some of our service providers may be located outside the European Economic Area (EEA). In such cases, we ensure an adequate level of protection through Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission, or other safeguards permitted under Chapter V of the GDPR.

6. Retention period

We store personal data only for as long as necessary to achieve the purposes for which it was collected, including:

  • Order and accounting data — 5 years from the end of the calendar year in which the tax obligation arose (as required by Polish tax law);
  • Account data — until you delete your account or request erasure;
  • Marketing data — until you withdraw your consent or object to processing;
  • Correspondence — for the duration necessary to handle the matter and a reasonable period thereafter to defend against claims;
  • Cookies — for the period defined in our Cookies Policy.

7. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR) — to obtain confirmation of whether we process your data and a copy of that data;
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR) — to correct inaccurate or incomplete data;
  • Right to erasure / "right to be forgotten" (Art. 17 GDPR) — to request deletion of your data;
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR);
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR) — to receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format;
  • Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR) — to object at any time to processing based on our legitimate interest, including for direct marketing;
  • Right to withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent (Art. 7(3) GDPR);
  • Right not to be subject to automated decision-making, including profiling (Art. 22 GDPR).

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info@qureprotein.com. We will respond within one month of receiving your request (this period may be extended by two further months for complex requests, in accordance with Art. 12(3) GDPR).

8. Right to lodge a complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority. In Poland, the supervisory authority is:

President of the Personal Data Protection Office (Prezes Urzędu Ochrony Danych Osobowych)
ul. Stawki 2, 00-193 Warsaw
Website: uodo.gov.pl

9. Provision of data — voluntary or required

Providing your personal data is voluntary, but may be required to enter into and perform a contract (e.g. to place an order or create an account). Failure to provide data necessary for the conclusion of a contract will make it impossible to provide the relevant service.

10. Automated decision-making and profiling

We do not make decisions about you that are based solely on automated processing (including profiling) and that produce legal effects concerning you or similarly significantly affect you, within the meaning of Art. 22 GDPR.

11. Data security

We apply appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure the security of personal data, including encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS), access control, regular backups, and ongoing staff training. In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a high risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you without undue delay and notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, in accordance with Articles 33 and 34 GDPR.

12. Cookies and similar technologies

Our Website uses cookies and similar technologies. Detailed information about the cookies we use, their purpose, retention period, and how to manage your cookie preferences is set out in our Cookies Policy.

13. Children

Our Website is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe that we have collected personal data from a child without proper consent, please contact us so we can delete the data.

14. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time, for example to reflect changes in law or our practices. Each version will indicate the date of last update at the top. If we make significant changes, we will notify you by email (where required) or via a prominent notice on the Website.

15. Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or our processing of your personal data, please contact us at info@qureprotein.com.
