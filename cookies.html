Cookies Policy

Last updated: 13 June 2026

1. About this policy

This Cookies Policy explains how QURE Sp. z o.o., with its registered office in Warsaw, Poland ("QURE", "we", "our", "us") uses cookies and similar technologies on the website qureprotein.com ("Website"). It is compliant with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR), Directive 2002/58/EC (ePrivacy Directive), and the Polish Act of 16 July 2004 - Telecommunications Law (Journal of Laws of 2024, item 34, as amended), which implements Article 5(3) of the ePrivacy Directive in Poland.

By "cookies" we mean small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, smartphone) by your web browser when you visit a website. We also use similar technologies such as local storage and pixel tags, which we collectively refer to as "cookies" in this policy.

2. Legal basis

We store and access cookies on your device on the following legal basis:

  • Strictly necessary cookies — stored without your consent, on the basis of the exception in Art. 173(3)(2) of the Polish Telecommunications Law (cookies necessary for the provision of an information society service explicitly requested by the user).
  • All other cookies — only with your prior, freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent (Art. 4(11) and Art. 7 GDPR; Art. 173(2) of the Polish Telecommunications Law). You may withdraw your consent at any time, with the same ease as you gave it.

Some of the cookies above are set by third-party service providers, including providers established outside the European Economic Area (EEA). Where this occurs, transfers are based on Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission or other safeguards permitted under Chapter V of the GDPR. You can find each provider's privacy policy on their respective websites.

5. Managing your cookie preferences

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time by:

  • Clicking the button to reopen our consent banner;

6. Consequences of withdrawing consent

If you reject non-essential cookies or withdraw your consent, you may still use the Website, but some features may be limited (for example, language preferences may not persist, or content may not be personalised). Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.

7. Personal data and cookies

Some cookies may collect information that, alone or combined with other data, qualifies as personal data under the GDPR. For details on how we process such data, please refer to our Privacy Policy.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookies Policy to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. The date of last update is shown at the top of this page. Significant changes will be communicated via a banner on the Website.
